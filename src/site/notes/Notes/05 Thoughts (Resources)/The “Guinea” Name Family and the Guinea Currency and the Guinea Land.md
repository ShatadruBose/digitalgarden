# The “Guinea” Name Family and the Guinea Currency

## Abstract

Several places and terms look related because they contain the word **Guinea**, but they are not all the same thing. **Guinea**, **Guinea-Bissau**, **Equatorial Guinea**, and **Papua New Guinea** are countries whose names include "Guinea" for historical and colonial reasons. **Guyana**, **Suriname**, and **French Guiana** belong to the related but distinct **Guiana** name family. The **Guinean franc (GNF)** is the modern currency of the country **Guinea** only. The old **guinea** was a historical British gold coin and is unrelated to the modern Guinean franc.

---

# 1. The Origin of the Name "Guinea"

The name **Guinea** originated from European explorers during the 15th century. They used it to describe a large portion of the West African coast south of the Sahara. The exact linguistic origin is uncertain, but it may have come from Berber or Portuguese adaptations of local names.

Over time, European colonial powers applied the name to several different territories around the world.

---

# 2. The Guinea Countries

## Guinea

- **Official Name:** Republic of Guinea
- **Continent:** Africa
- **Capital:** Conakry
- **Currency:** Guinean Franc (GNF)

This is the country from which the modern **Guinean franc** gets its name.

---

## Guinea-Bissau

- **Official Name:** Republic of Guinea-Bissau
- **Continent:** Africa
- **Capital:** Bissau
- **Currency:** West African CFA Franc (XOF)

"Bissau" was added to distinguish it from neighboring Guinea.

---

## Equatorial Guinea

- **Official Name:** Republic of Equatorial Guinea
- **Continent:** Africa
- **Capital:** Malabo
- **Currency:** Central African CFA Franc (XAF)

The name comes from:
- Guinea region
- Near the Equator

---

## Papua New Guinea

- **Official Name:** Independent State of Papua New Guinea
- **Continent:** Oceania

Currency:
- Papua New Guinean Kina (PGK)

European explorers named the island **New Guinea** because its people reminded them of those living in the African Guinea region.

# 4. Guinea vs Guiana

Although they sound similar, they are different historical names.

| Guinea | Guiana |
|---------|---------|
| Mainly Africa | Mainly South America |
| Several independent countries | One country, one overseas department, one former colony |
| Origin linked to West African coast | Indigenous name for northern South America |

---

# 5. The Guinean Franc (GNF)

Official Currency of:

- Guinea

ISO Code:

GNF

Symbol:

FG

Central Bank:

Central Bank of the Republic of Guinea

Used Only In:

- Guinea

It is **NOT** used in Guinea-Bissau, Equatorial Guinea, Papua New Guinea, Guyana, Suriname, or French Guiana.

---

# 6. The Historical Guinea Coin

The **guinea** was an old British gold coin.

Facts:

- Introduced: 1663
- Country: England / Great Britain
- Metal: Gold
- Value: 21 Shillings
- Decimal Equivalent: £1.05

It was commonly used for:

- Horse sales
- Luxury goods
- Professional fees
- Fine art

The coin disappeared from circulation during the early 19th century.

# 9. Is There a "Guinea Land"?

Historically, Europeans sometimes referred to the **Guinea Coast** or **Guinea Region** of West Africa.

However:

- There has never been a sovereign country called **Guinea Land**.
- There has never been a political union including Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Equatorial Guinea, Papua New Guinea, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.
- There has never been one currency shared by all of these places.

The only common feature is that their names contain **Guinea** or **Guiana** due to historical naming traditions.

---

# 10. Summary

- Guinea is a country in West Africa.
- Guinea-Bissau and Equatorial Guinea are separate African countries.
- Papua New Guinea is in Oceania.
- Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana belong to the Guiana region of South America.
- The **Guinean Franc (GNF)** is used only in Guinea.
- The historical British **guinea** was a gold coin worth **21 shillings** and is unrelated to the modern Guinean franc.
- There is no unified "Guinea Land" or common Guinea currency shared by all seven places.
