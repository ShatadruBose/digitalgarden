# 🪐 Gordon — Rogue Super-Terrestrial Planet (The Gordonators' World)

## 1) Quick Facts
- **Type:** Rogue ultra-massive rocky planet  
- **Mass:** **7 × MJ = 1.3286 × 10^28 kg**  
- **Status:** Free-floating / no host star (rogue)  
- **Surface:** Solid rocky crust with Earth-like composition from 0–100 km  
- **Total atmosphere height (above surface):** **100,000 km**

---

## 2) Size & Bulk Properties (estimates)
- **Estimated radius:** **15,000 – 22,000 km** (compact because of extreme mass)  
- **Estimated surface gravity:** **~150 – 300 g** (strong range due to radius uncertainty)  
- **Estimated escape velocity:** **~300 km/s** (order of a few 10^5 m/s)  
- **Magnetosphere (estimated):** **~3 – 10 million km** (strong, driven by a large, conductive core)

> Notes: these are order-of-magnitude astrophysical estimates consistent with a rocky body of mass 7×MJ that retains a very compact radius.

---

## 3) Surface & Interior
- **Upper 0–100 km:** Composition and density **match terrestrial (Earth-like) crust and upper mantle** — silicates, oxides, iron-nickel components. This is the only zone with Earth-like density and composition.  
- **Below ~100 km:** Progressive increase of pressure and density. The mantle and core will be extremely compressed compared to terrestrial examples, driving intense tectonics and mantle convection.  
- **Geological activity:** Massive tectonics, frequent/huge volcanism, and strong mantle plumes expected.

---

## 4) Atmospheric Structure — LAYERED
All layers measured from the **surface upward**.

1. **Layer 1 — 0 to 100 km**  
   - Earth-like density and composition (N₂, O₂, H₂O vapor, trace gases).  
   - Physical conditions potentially suitable for life and familiar weather (clouds, rain, winds) within this band.

2. **Layer 2 — 100 to 500 km**  
   - Rapidly increasing pressure and temperature relative to Layer 1.  
   - Thick cloud bands, strong convection cells, large-scale storms.

3. **Layer 3 — 500 to 1000 km**  
   - Very dense and highly compressed compared with the top layers (density increases significantly below 1000 km).  
   - Possible supercritical fluid behavior for some atmospheric constituents; extreme winds and pressure gradients.

4. **Layer 4 — 1000 to 10,000 km**  
   - Transition to rarer gas regime but still far denser than typical planetary upper atmospheres.  
   - Ionization begins to increase; persistent charged particle populations; intense lightning and electrical phenomena.

5. **Layer 5 — 10,000 to 50,000 km**  
   - Mostly tenuous neutral gas with increasing ionization and plasma interactions.  
   - Contains strong auroral zones where magnetic field channels particles.

6. **Layer 6 — 50,000 to 100,000 km**  
   - Very thin exospheric region merging into interstellar space; dominated by ionized atoms and magnetospheric plasma.  
   - Radiation belts and trapped charged-particle populations extend through this layer.

## 5) Rings & Satellites
- **Ring System**
  - **Inner Ring:** 5,000 – 25,000 km (dense particulate ring)  
  - **Middle Ring:** 50,000 – 500,000 km (broad debris belt)  
  - **Outer Ring:** 1,000,000 – 15,000,000 km (massive extended ring system)

- **Moon System**
  - **Mini Moon Ring:** 25,000 – 50,000 km — **10 moons** (small, irregular)  
  - **Main Moon Ring:** 500,000 – 1,000,000 km — **150 moons** (diverse sizes; complex resonances)

_Total moons: 160._

---

## 6) Energy Budget & Habitability Notes (rogue context)
- **Primary internal heat sources:** radioactive decay, residual formation heat, and tidal heating from the dense moon population.  
- **Surface habitability (0–100 km):** Conceivable if local conditions (temperature, pressure, liquid water) are right — internal heat + dense atmosphere can sustain temperate zones despite absence of stellar insolation.  
- **Lighting:** Permanently starless sky; bioluminescence, auroras, and reflected light from rings/moons would dominate night/day illumination.

---

## 7) Sky & Visuals
- Rings would appear enormous (subtending large angles) from many surface points.  
- Moons in the main ring could produce spectacular, frequent eclipses and tidal effects.  
- Auroral curtains on a planetary scale, visible across the dark interstellar background.
