# The Miniature Civilization Beneath The Table

## Overview

Hidden beneath the top shelf of an ordinary human table exists an entire microscopic civilization.  
The gap is only about **1 inch tall** to humans, making it useless for storing most human objects.  
Dust gathers there. Shadows dominate it. Forgotten particles drift silently through the darkness.

But for them?

It is an entire world.

A vast horizontal megacity stretching beneath the table surface like a hidden continent.

These beings are known as the **Minari**.

They resemble humans almost perfectly in anatomy, emotion, society, and intelligence — except they are scaled at **1:100** relative to humans.

---

# Scale System

## Relative Scale

The Minari world operates at a strict **1:100 dimensional ratio**.

| Human Scale | Minari Equivalent |
|---|---|
| 1 centimeter | 1 meter |
| 1 inch gap | 2.54 meter ceiling |
| Pencil dust | Large rocks |
| Human hair | Thick cable or tree trunk |
| Water droplet | Massive floating sphere |
| Crumbs | Food mountains |
| Finger tap | Earthquake |
| Smartphone vibration | Seismic storm |

To the Minari:

- The underside of the table is like the sky.
- The table legs are colossal support towers.
- Wood grain appears like enormous terrain ridges.
- Tiny scratches in furniture become canyons.
- Dust particles become drifting boulders.

# Neurochips

## Neural Integration

Every Minari is born with microscopic **NeuroCore Chips** embedded inside their brain.

These are not artificial implants added later.

They are biological-technological hybrid organs inherited genetically.

The neurochips:
- Enhance intelligence
- Process information rapidly
- Enable memory storage
- Allow wireless communication
- Stabilize magic usage
- Control electricity flow

Their thoughts can partially synchronize with nearby devices.

Some elite Minari can:
- Interface with human electronics
- Read electromagnetic signals
- Detect WiFi waves
- Interpret screen light patterns

To them:
WiFi is like invisible weather.

# Nosedeenians

The Minari civilization uses a specialized bio-electric species known as the **Nosedeenians** as one of their primary energy-generation systems.

Nosedeenians are living electro-organic beings capable of naturally producing, storing, amplifying, and transferring electrical energy at extremely high efficiency relative to their scale. Entire districts, laboratories, portal gates, rail systems, and communication grids may be partially powered through Nosedeenian energy farms and cooperative electrical networks.

Their bodies are:
- Black and cylindrical
- Smooth but highly conductive
- Covered with glowing yellow, white, or green electrical accents
- Marked by a massive lightning-bolt symbol across the chest
- Marked on the back with large plus (+) and minus (-) battery-like symbols

Physical structure:
- Roughly 1 foot tall relative to Minari scale
- Pointed legs for stable conductive grounding
- Three fingers on each hand optimized for precision electrical handling
- Internal electro-core organs capable of continuous charge cycling

Their bodies naturally absorb:
- Static electricity
- Thermal gradients
- Electromagnetic radiation
- Stored capacitor energy
- Environmental electrical leakage

The electricity generated by Nosedeenians is:
- Extremely stable
- High-frequency
- Easily transferable into Minari systems
- Compatible with neurochip technologies

Some Nosedeenians voluntarily connect themselves into city power frameworks where they function as living generators, while others work as mobile energy carriers, battlefield power suppliers, or emergency electrical reserves.

Elite Nosedeenians called:
**Storm Conductors**

can generate electrical discharges powerful enough to overload entire miniature districts or activate unstable portal systems.

- Micro-rockets

- Inter-Studio transport systems

Rocket launch facilities are often hidden:
- inside cable tunnels,
- behind shelf supports,
- beneath drawers,

The Minari have mapped large portions of:
- the table ecosystem,
- nearby furniture regions,
- wall interiors,

- and hidden Studio portal sectors.

Their aerospace divisions study:
- airflow turbulence caused by humans,
- electromagnetic weather,
- thermal updrafts,
- static storms,

Advanced spacecraft can even travel through stabilized portal corridors connecting multiple Miniature Studios together.
