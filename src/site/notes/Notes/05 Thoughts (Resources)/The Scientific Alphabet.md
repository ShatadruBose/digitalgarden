# The Scientific Alphabet

A comprehensive reference of Latin and Greek letters across Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Astronomy, Engineering, and other sciences.

---

## Volume 1 — Latin Alphabet (A–Z, a–z)

### Chapter 1: A, a
* **A**: Ampere (current), Area, Mass number (physics), Adenine (biology), Vector potential (electromagnetism), Helmholtz free energy (thermodynamics), Work (physics - occasionally).
* **a**: Acceleration, Semi-major axis (astronomy), Atto- (prefix $10^{-18}$), Activity (chemistry), Absorption coefficient.

### Chapter 2: B, b
* **B**: Magnetic field (flux density), Susceptance (electronics), Boron (chemistry), Bel (acoustics), Radiance (optics), Bottom quantum number (particle physics).
* **b**: Impact parameter (scattering), Barn (area/cross-section), Galactic latitude (astronomy), Molality (chemistry), Bottom quark.

### Chapter 3: C, c
* **C**: Capacitance, Coulomb (charge), Carbon (chemistry), Heat capacity (thermodynamics), Cytosine (biology), Integration constant (math).
* **c**: Speed of light in a vacuum ($2.99 \times 10^8$ m/s), Centi- (prefix $10^{-2}$), Specific heat capacity, Charm quark.

### Chapter 4: D, d
* **D**: Diffusion coefficient, Electric displacement field, Deuterium, Drag (aerodynamics), Dimension (math), Absorbed dose (radiation).
* **d**: Differential operator (calculus), Distance, Diameter, Deci- (prefix $10^{-1}$), Down quark, Days.

### Chapter 5: E, e
* **E**: Energy, Electric field, Expected value (statistics), Young's modulus (materials), Illuminance, Exa- (prefix $10^{18}$).
* **e**: Elementary charge ($1.602 \times 10^{-19}$ C), Euler's number (2.718...), Electron, Eccentricity (orbital), Restitution coefficient.

### Chapter 6: F, f
* **F**: Force, Faraday constant, Farad (capacitance), Fluorine, Fahrenheit, Helmholtz free energy, F-distribution (statistics).
* **f**: Frequency, Focal length (optics), Friction coefficient, Femto- (prefix $10^{-15}$), Function (math), Fugacity (thermodynamics).

### Chapter 7: G, g
* **G**: Gravitational constant ($6.674 \times 10^{-11}$), Gibbs free energy, Conductance (electronics), Shear modulus, Giga- (prefix $10^9$), Guanine (biology).
* **g**: Acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^2), Gram, Gluon, Metric tensor (relativity), Landé g-factor.

### Chapter 8: H, h
* **H**: Enthalpy, Magnetic field strength, Hydrogen, Hamiltonian (quantum mechanics), Henry (inductance), Hubble constant.
* **h**: Planck constant ($6.626 \times 10^{-34}$ J s), Height, Hecto- (prefix $10^2$), Heat transfer coefficient, Hour.

### Chapter 9: I, i
* **I**: Electric current, Moment of inertia, Intensity (optics/acoustics), Iodine, Identity matrix (math), Luminous intensity.
* **i**: Imaginary unit ($\sqrt{-1}$), Index variable, Unit vector (x-axis), Interest rate (economics).

### Chapter 10: J, j
* **J**: Joule (energy), Current density, Angular momentum, Bessel function of first kind, Jacobian matrix.
* **j**: Imaginary unit (engineering/electronics), Unit vector (y-axis), Jerk (rate of change of acceleration).

### Chapter 11: K, k
* **K**: Kelvin (temperature), Potassium (chemistry), Kinetic energy, Equilibrium constant (chemistry), Bulk modulus.
* **k**: Boltzmann constant ($1.38 \times 10^{-23}$ J/K), Spring constant (Hooke's Law), Wavenumber, Kilo- (prefix $10^3$), Rate constant (chemistry), Unit vector (z-axis).

### Chapter 12: L, l
* **L**: Inductance (henry), Angular momentum, Luminosity, Liter, Lagrangian (mechanics), Avogadro constant (alternative).
* **l**: Length, Azimuthal quantum number, Mean free path, Liter (alternative).

### Chapter 13: M, m
* **M**: Mass, Molar mass, Magnetization, Mega- (prefix $10^6$), Mach number, Molarity, Bending moment.
* **m**: Mass, Meter, Milli- (prefix $10^{-3}$), Molality, Magnetic quantum number, Slope (math).

### Chapter 14: N, n
* **N**: Newton (force), Nitrogen, Normal force, Number of particles, Avogadro's number ($N_A$), Neutron.
* **n**: Amount of substance (moles), Refractive index, Nano- (prefix $10^{-9}$), Principal quantum number, Neutron (alternative).

### Chapter 15: O, o
* **O**: Oxygen, Origin (math), Big O notation (computer science/math).
* **o**: Little o notation (math). (Rarely used in physics to avoid confusion with zero).

### Chapter 16: P, p
* **P**: Power, Pressure, Phosphorus, Probability, Polarization, Peta- (prefix $10^{15}$), Momentum (sometimes).
* **p**: Momentum, Pressure, Proton, Pico- (prefix $10^{-12}$), Dipole moment, p-value (statistics), Pitch (aerodynamics).

### Chapter 17: Q, q
* **Q**: Electric charge, Heat (thermodynamics), Volumetric flow rate, Quality factor, Reaction quotient.
* **q**: Electric charge, Heat, Dynamic pressure, Generalized coordinate (mechanics).

### Chapter 18: R, r
* **R**: Resistance, Ideal gas constant, Radius, Real numbers ($\mathbb{R}$), Rydberg constant, Roentgen.
* **r**: Radius, Position vector, Correlation coefficient (statistics), Interest rate.

### Chapter 19: S, s
* **S**: Entropy, Sulfur, Siemens (conductance), Surface area, Spin, Action (physics), StrANGENESS (particle physics).
* **s**: Second (time), Displacement, Specific entropy, Standard deviation (sample), s-quark.

### Chapter 20: T, t
* **T**: Temperature, Period (time), Tesla (magnetic field), Kinetic energy, Tera- (prefix $10^{12}$), Thymine (biology).
* **t**: Time, Student's t-distribution, Top quark, Celsius temperature (sometimes).

### Chapter 21: U, u
* **U**: Internal energy, Potential energy, Uranium, Voltage (European convention).
* **u**: Atomic mass unit, Velocity (often initial), Up quark, Specific internal energy.

### Chapter 22: V, v
* **V**: Volume, Voltage, Vanadium, Potential energy, Volt, Variance (statistics).
* **v**: Velocity, Specific volume, Kinematic viscosity.

### Chapter 23: W, w
* **W**: Work, Watt, Tungsten (Wolfram), Weight, W boson, Lambert W function.
* **w**: Velocity, Width, Weight (sometimes).

### Chapter 24: X, x
* **X**: Reactance (electronics), Random variable (statistics), Halogen (chemistry).
* **x**: Position (x-axis), Unknown variable, Mole fraction (chemistry).

### Chapter 25: Y, y
* **Y**: Yttrium, Admittance (electronics), Young's modulus, Luminance, Bessel function of second kind.
* **y**: Position (y-axis), Dependent variable.

### Chapter 26: Z, z
* **Z**: Atomic number, Impedance (electronics), Partition function (thermodynamics), Integers ($\mathbb{Z}$), Z boson.
* **z**: Position (z-axis), Complex variable ($x + iy$), Redshift (astronomy), z-score (statistics).

## Volume 2 — Greek Alphabet (Α–Ω, α–ω)

### Chapter 27: Alpha (Α, α)
* **Α**: Alpha (rarely used due to similarity with Latin A).
* **α**: Alpha particle/radiation, Fine-structure constant, Angular acceleration, Coefficient of linear expansion, Type I error (statistics), Degree of dissociation (chemistry).

### Chapter 28: Beta (Β, β)
* **Β**: Beta function (math).
* **β**: Beta particle, Velocity as a fraction of c ($v/c$), Thermodynamic beta ($1/kT$), Type II error (statistics), Current gain (transistors).

### Chapter 29: Gamma (Γ, γ)
* **Γ**: Gamma function (math), Christoffel symbols (relativity), Torque, Reflection coefficient.
* **γ**: Gamma rays/photon, Lorentz factor (relativity), Heat capacity ratio, Specific weight, Shear strain, Gyromagnetic ratio.

### Chapter 30: Delta (Δ, δ)
* **Δ**: Change in macroscopic variable (difference), Laplace operator, Discriminant, Delta particle.
* **δ**: Dirac delta function, Kronecker delta, Variation (calculus of variations), Skin depth, Chemical shift (NMR).

### Chapter 31: Epsilon (Ε, ε/ϵ)
* **Ε**: Expected value (rarely, usually Latin E).
* **ε**: Permittivity, Strain (mechanics), Emissivity, Electromotive force (EMF), Machine epsilon (computer science), Arbitrarily small positive quantity (math).

### Chapter 32: Zeta (Ζ, ζ)
* **Ζ**: Zeta (rare).
* **ζ**: Riemann zeta function, Damping ratio (engineering), Zeta potential (chemistry), Vorticity (fluid mechanics).

### Chapter 33: Eta (Η, η)
* **Η**: Eta theorem (rare).
* **η**: Dynamic viscosity, Efficiency (thermodynamics/engineering), Minkowski metric tensor (relativity), Overpotential (electrochemistry).

### Chapter 34: Theta (Θ, θ/ϑ)
* **Θ**: Temperature, Heaviside step function, Big Theta notation.
* **θ**: Angle (polar coordinates), Temperature (alternative to T), Potential temperature (meteorology), Angle of incidence.

### Chapter 35: Iota (Ι, ι)
* **Ι**: Iota (rare).
* **ι**: Inclusion map (math), Index generator (APL programming).

### Chapter 36: Kappa (Κ, κ)
* **Κ**: Kappa (rare).
* **κ**: Curvature, Thermal conductivity, Dielectric constant, Compressibility, Coupling coefficient.

### Chapter 37: Lambda (Λ, λ)
* **Λ**: Cosmological constant, Lambda baryon, Diagonal matrix of eigenvalues, Permeance.
* **λ**: Wavelength, Eigenvalue, Decay constant (radioactivity), Mean free path, Thermal conductivity, Lagrange multiplier.

### Chapter 38: Mu (Μ, μ)
* **Μ**: Mu (rare).
* **μ**: Micro- (prefix $10^{-6}$), Permeability (electromagnetism), Friction coefficient, Chemical potential, Dynamic viscosity, Magnetic moment, Mean (statistics), Muon.

### Chapter 39: Nu (Ν, ν)
* **Ν**: Nu (rare).
* **ν**: Frequency, Kinematic viscosity, Poisson's ratio, Neutrino, Degrees of freedom (statistics), Stoichiometric coefficient.

### Chapter 40: Xi (Ξ, ξ)
* **Ξ**: Grand canonical ensemble partition function, Xi baryon.
* **ξ**: Extent of reaction (chemistry), Damping ratio, Coherence length, Universal spatial coordinate.

### Chapter 41: Omicron (Ο, ο)
* **Ο**: Big O notation (historic).
* **ο**: Little o notation (historic). (Rarely used, usually Latin O).

### Chapter 42: Pi (Π, π)
* **Π**: Product operator (math), Osmotic pressure, Peltier coefficient.
* **π**: Pi constant (3.14159...), Pion (particle), Parity (physics), Profit (economics).

### Chapter 43: Rho (Ρ, ρ)
* **Ρ**: Rho (rare).
* **ρ**: Density, Electrical resistivity, Charge density, Radial coordinate, Correlation coefficient, Density matrix (quantum mechanics).

### Chapter 44: Sigma (Σ, σ/ς)
* **Σ**: Summation operator, Covariance matrix, Surface charge density.
* **σ**: Standard deviation, Stefan-Boltzmann constant, Stress (mechanics), Electrical conductivity, Cross-section (physics), Pauli spin matrices.

### Chapter 45: Tau (Τ, τ)
* **Τ**: Tau (rare).
* **τ**: Torque, Shear stress, Time constant, Proper time (relativity), Tau lepton, Transmittance.

### Chapter 46: Upsilon (Υ, υ)
* **Υ**: Upsilon meson.
* **υ**: Frequency (rare variant), Specific volume (variant).

### Chapter 47: Phi (Φ, φ/ϕ)
* **Φ**: Magnetic flux, Electric flux, Work function, Cumulative distribution function.
* **φ**: Golden ratio (1.618...), Angle (azimuthal), Electric potential, Wavefunction (quantum mechanics), Porosity.

### Chapter 48: Chi (Χ, χ)
* **Χ**: Chi (rare).
* **χ**: Electronegativity, Magnetic susceptibility, Chi-squared distribution (statistics), Mole fraction.

### Chapter 49: Psi (Ψ, ψ)
* **Ψ**: Wavefunction (quantum mechanics, multi-particle), Water potential (biology).
* **ψ**: Wavefunction (quantum mechanics, single particle), Stream function (fluid dynamics), Yaw angle.

### Chapter 50: Omega (Ω, ω)
* **Ω**: Ohm (resistance), Solid angle, Density parameter (cosmology), Sample space (statistics), Omega baryon.
* **ω**: Angular velocity, Angular frequency, Argument of periapsis (astronomy), Vorticity.

## Appendix I: Historical Origins
* **Latin Alphabet:** Derived from the Etruscan and Greek alphabets, standardized by the Romans. Used as the primary script for physical units and algebraic variables.
* **Greek Alphabet:** Adopted from the Phoenician alphabet. Brought into mathematics by early Greek mathematicians (Euclid, Archimedes) and revived during the European Renaissance for modern scientific notation to provide a distinct set of variables separate from Latin.
